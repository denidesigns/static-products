Don’t be fooled by the amazing beachside location - this is not a tourist trap like the surrounding restaurants. Can Fisher specialises in seafood with a particular focus on paella. This place has a great vibe on the weekend; both locals and tourists book way ahead to secure their spot in the sun and wash down their famous dishes with local wines. The prices are considerably higher than the average restaurant in the area, but you are paying for the freshest seafood Barcelona can offer and amazing service.

This is probably the fanciest chicken rotisserie you will ever visit. Chez coco specialises in roast chicken - just pick your favourite seasoning, sauce, and sides and you’re set for one of the best poultry meals in town. The interior and crowd is upscale; the wine list is impeccable. For a more casual visit, they have a trendy yet relaxed backyard with live music and an Aperol spritz bar during the summer season. You are pretty much guaranteed to be surrounded by only locals at this spot.
